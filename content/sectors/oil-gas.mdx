## Overview

Nigeria is Africa's largest crude oil producer and a founding member of OPEC. The oil and gas sector is the country's primary source of foreign exchange earnings and a significant contributor to government revenue, even as the broader economy diversifies. On the NGX, the sector is represented by a mix of indigenous exploration and production (E&P) companies and downstream distributors, including Seplat Energy, Aradel Holdings, TotalEnergies Marketing Nigeria, Oando, and Conoil.

The passage of the Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) in 2021 marked a watershed for the sector, creating a new fiscal and regulatory framework designed to attract investment, improve transparency, and support host community development. The PIA replaced decades-old legislation and established the Nigerian Upstream Petroleum Regulatory Commission (NUPRC) and the Nigerian Midstream and Downstream Petroleum Regulatory Authority (NMDPRA).

The commissioning of the Dangote Refinery, Africa's largest single-train refinery, has fundamentally altered the downstream landscape. By refining crude domestically, Nigeria is reducing its dependence on imported petroleum products, reshaping fuel pricing dynamics, and creating new opportunities across the value chain.

## Key Drivers

- **Global crude oil prices**: Brent crude prices directly influence upstream revenue and profitability. OPEC+ production quotas also determine export volumes.
- **Petroleum Industry Act (PIA)**: The new fiscal regime, including royalty structures and tax incentives, shapes investment returns for upstream operators.
- **Dangote Refinery operations**: Domestic refining capacity is changing product sourcing, pricing, and margins for downstream players.
- **Production volumes**: Nigeria's ability to meet its OPEC quota depends on pipeline security, field maintenance, and new well development.
- **Energy transition**: Growing global ESG focus and the shift toward renewables create long-term strategic questions for fossil fuel producers.

## Key Risks

- **Oil price volatility**: A sustained drop in crude prices compresses upstream earnings and government revenue simultaneously.
- **Pipeline vandalism and oil theft**: Insecurity in the Niger Delta region continues to disrupt production and increase operating costs.
- **Regulatory uncertainty**: Disagreements over PIA implementation, host community fund administration, and licensing terms can delay investment decisions.
- **FX and subsidy policy**: Changes in fuel subsidy policy and naira-dollar dynamics affect downstream margins and consumer prices.

## Notable Events

- Seplat Energy completed its acquisition of ExxonMobil's shallow-water assets, significantly expanding its production base and reserve portfolio.
- Aradel Holdings listed on the NGX following a restructuring, becoming one of the largest indigenous E&P companies by market capitalisation.
- The Dangote Refinery began processing crude in 2024, with phased ramp-up continuing through 2025 and into 2026.

## Outlook

The oil and gas sector is at an inflection point. The PIA is attracting fresh upstream investment, domestic refining is reducing import dependency, and indigenous operators are taking on larger roles as international oil companies divest onshore assets. However, global energy transition pressures and local security challenges remain headwinds. Investors should monitor crude price trends, OPEC+ policy, refinery output data, and PIA implementation for signals on sector direction.
