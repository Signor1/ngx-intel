## Overview

Nigeria faces one of the largest housing deficits in the world, estimated at over 20 million units, a gap that grows by roughly 900,000 units annually as population growth and urbanisation outpace new construction. Despite this enormous structural demand, the listed real estate sector on the NGX remains small and illiquid, with UPDC (formerly UAC Property Development Company) and LivingTrust Mortgage Bank (LIVINGTRUST) among the few actively traded names.

The sector encompasses property development, real estate investment trusts (REITs), mortgage banking, and facilities management. UPDC has historically focused on residential and commercial property development in Lagos, while LivingTrust operates as a mortgage bank providing housing finance to retail and institutional clients. The NGX also hosts a small number of REITs, though market adoption has been slow compared to more developed markets.

The real estate sector's underrepresentation on the exchange reflects broader structural barriers: land title issues under the Land Use Act, high construction costs driven by imported building materials, limited long-term mortgage availability, and interest rates that make homeownership unaffordable for most Nigerians. The mortgage-to-GDP ratio in Nigeria sits below 1%, compared to 20-30% in South Africa and over 50% in developed economies.

## Key Drivers

- **Housing deficit**: The sheer scale of unmet housing demand provides a decades-long growth opportunity for developers and mortgage banks that can serve the affordable housing segment.
- **Urbanisation**: Lagos, Abuja, and other major cities are growing rapidly, driving demand for residential, commercial, and mixed-use developments.
- **Mortgage market development**: Any improvement in mortgage availability, interest rate affordability, or government housing schemes directly benefits mortgage banks and developers.
- **REIT adoption**: Greater investor awareness and regulatory support for REITs could channel institutional capital into the property market and provide new listed investment vehicles.
- **Diaspora demand**: Nigerians living abroad represent a significant source of demand for residential property, particularly in Lagos and Abuja.

## Key Risks

- **High interest rates**: Elevated lending rates make mortgages unaffordable for the mass market, constraining effective demand.
- **Land title and regulatory challenges**: Complex land acquisition processes, the Land Use Act, and multiple layers of government approval increase development timelines and costs.
- **Construction cost inflation**: Rising prices for cement, steel, and other building materials, compounded by naira depreciation on imported inputs, push up development costs.
- **Low liquidity**: With few listed companies and low trading volumes, the sector offers limited entry and exit flexibility for investors.

## Notable Events

- UPDC underwent a restructuring, with its parent company (UACN) divesting its stake, creating a more focused and independent property company.
- LivingTrust Mortgage Bank expanded its loan book, benefiting from increased demand for housing finance despite the challenging interest rate environment.
- The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) continued to promote REITs as a vehicle for real estate investment, though listing activity remained modest.

## Outlook

The real estate sector's long-term potential is underpinned by demographics and urbanisation, but unlocking that potential requires structural reforms in land administration, mortgage market development, and affordable housing policy. For investors, the sector offers high upside but demands patience and tolerance for illiquidity. Any meaningful policy breakthrough on housing finance or REIT regulation could be a powerful catalyst for rerating.
