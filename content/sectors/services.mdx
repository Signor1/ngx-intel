## Overview

The services sector on the NGX is a catch-all category that groups companies operating in non-manufacturing, non-financial service industries, including education, publishing, distribution, and general trading. The sector is small by market capitalisation and trading volume relative to banking or industrial goods, but it captures an important slice of Nigeria's economic transition from commodity dependence toward a services-driven economy. Key listed names include Learn Africa (LEARNAFRICA) and R.T. Briscoe (RTBRISCOE).

Learn Africa, formerly known as Longman Nigeria, is one of Nigeria's leading educational publishers, producing textbooks, workbooks, and supplementary learning materials for primary and secondary schools. The company benefits from Nigeria's large and growing school-age population and periodic government curriculum reviews that trigger new textbook adoption cycles. The Universal Basic Education (UBE) programme and state-level education procurement provide recurring demand, though payment cycles from government buyers can be long and unpredictable.

R.T. Briscoe operates in engineering, automobile distribution, and property management. The company has historically represented brands such as Toyota in Nigeria, though competitive dynamics in the auto distribution market have evolved. Its diversified service portfolio reflects the broad nature of the sector itself.

The services sector's small size on the NGX belies the importance of services in the Nigerian economy, which accounts for over 50% of GDP. Many of the largest services businesses -- in fintech, logistics, media, and professional services -- remain unlisted or venture-backed, representing a pipeline of potential future listings.

## Key Drivers

- **Education spending**: Government and household expenditure on education drives demand for textbooks, learning materials, and related publishing services.
- **Curriculum changes**: National curriculum reviews and new textbook approval lists create demand cycles that benefit publishers like Learn Africa.
- **Population demographics**: Over 40% of Nigeria's population is under 15, generating sustained demand for educational materials and youth-focused services.
- **Economic diversification**: As Nigeria's economy shifts toward services, companies in distribution, logistics, and professional services stand to benefit from the structural reallocation of economic activity.
- **Digital transformation**: E-learning platforms, digital textbooks, and online distribution channels represent growth opportunities for forward-looking service companies.

## Key Risks

- **Government procurement delays**: Education publishers are exposed to slow and unpredictable payment cycles from state and federal government agencies.
- **Low liquidity**: Thin trading volumes make it difficult for institutional investors to build or exit positions without moving prices significantly.
- **Competition from informal channels**: Pirated textbooks and unofficial reproductions undermine sales volumes and pricing power for legitimate publishers.
- **Limited sector scale**: The small number of listed companies restricts portfolio diversification within the sector.

## Notable Events

- Learn Africa secured inclusion on several state textbook adoption lists, supporting revenue visibility for upcoming academic cycles.
- R.T. Briscoe explored strategic options to refocus its business portfolio and improve profitability after several challenging years.
- The Lagos State government's push to digitise educational materials highlighted both the opportunity and competitive threat facing traditional publishers.

## Outlook

The services sector is a niche but evolving corner of the NGX. Learn Africa's positioning in educational publishing gives it exposure to Nigeria's demographic dividend, while R.T. Briscoe's diversified services model faces the ongoing challenge of finding sustainable competitive advantages. The sector would benefit enormously from new listings by high-growth Nigerian service companies in technology, logistics, and professional services. For now, it remains a low-liquidity segment best suited for investors with specific thematic convictions rather than broad sector allocation.
