## Overview

The Information and Communications Technology (ICT) sector has emerged as one of the fastest-growing segments of the Nigerian economy, contributing over 18% of GDP and serving as the infrastructure layer upon which digital finance, e-commerce, and the broader tech ecosystem operate. On the NGX, the sector is anchored by two telecom heavyweights: MTN Nigeria (MTNN) and Airtel Africa (AIRTELAFRI), which together serve over 150 million subscribers and rank among the largest companies on the exchange by market capitalisation.

Nigeria's telecom industry has undergone a remarkable transformation since the liberalisation of the sector in 2001. Mobile penetration now exceeds 80%, but data penetration and broadband access remain relatively low, providing a long runway for growth. The ongoing rollout of 4G LTE infrastructure and the early stages of 5G deployment are expanding the range of services operators can deliver, from high-speed internet to enterprise solutions and mobile financial services.

The Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) regulates the sector, managing spectrum allocation, quality-of-service standards, and interconnection rates. Regulatory decisions on spectrum pricing, tower sharing, and SIM registration requirements have material impacts on operator economics and capital expenditure plans.

## Key Drivers

- **Data consumption growth**: Smartphone adoption and the expansion of digital services (streaming, social media, mobile banking) are driving rapid growth in data revenue, which is displacing voice as the primary revenue source.
- **Mobile money and fintech**: Telecom operators, through Payment Service Bank (PSB) licences, are entering the financial services space. MTN's MoMo PSB aims to capture a share of Nigeria's massive unbanked population.
- **5G rollout**: Early 5G deployment in major cities opens new enterprise and consumer revenue streams, including fixed wireless access and IoT connectivity.
- **Tower infrastructure**: The expansion and co-location of telecom towers support broader coverage while enabling operators to manage capital expenditure through tower-sharing arrangements.
- **Population and youth demographics**: A young, digitally native population drives organic subscriber and data usage growth.

## Key Risks

- **Regulatory and taxation pressure**: Multiple taxation across federal, state, and local government levels, plus sector-specific levies, squeeze margins. Spectrum licence renewals represent significant lump-sum capital outlays.
- **Infrastructure costs**: Powering telecom sites with diesel generators in the absence of reliable grid electricity is a major operating expense.
- **Foreign exchange exposure**: Equipment imports, dividend repatriation, and foreign-currency-denominated obligations expose operators to naira volatility.
- **Security and vandalism**: Theft of tower equipment, fibre cuts, and insecurity in certain regions increase operating costs and service disruption.

## Notable Events

- MTN Nigeria launched its MoMo Payment Service Bank, marking the company's formal entry into financial services and mobile money.
- Airtel Africa continued its strong growth trajectory, with data and mobile money revenues outpacing voice across its African footprint.
- The NCC awarded 5G spectrum licences, with MTN and Mafab Communications securing the initial allocations for commercial deployment.

## Outlook

The ICT sector is a structural growth story on the NGX. Data demand is growing at double-digit rates, mobile money presents a large untapped opportunity, and 5G will unlock new use cases over the medium term. Valuation premiums relative to other NGX sectors reflect this growth potential. Investors should monitor regulatory developments, spectrum costs, and the pace of mobile money adoption as key indicators of sector trajectory.
