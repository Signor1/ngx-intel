Imagine you are a school principal checking exam results. If only 5 out of 200 students scored above 70%, you would not call it a good performance — even if those 5 scored 100%. But if 150 out of 200 students scored above 70%, that is genuinely impressive. **Market breadth** applies the same logic to the stock market: it tells you whether a market rally or decline is widespread or driven by just a few stocks.

**Market breadth** measures the number of stocks participating in a market move. It answers the question: "Is the entire market moving up, or are just a handful of big stocks dragging the index higher?"

## Why Market Breadth Matters

The NGX All-Share Index (ASI) is weighted by market capitalisation. This means that large companies like **Dangote Cement**, **MTN Nigeria**, and **BUA Cement** have an outsized influence on the index. The ASI could go up on a day when most stocks actually declined — if the big names rallied enough to pull the index higher.

Market breadth looks beneath the surface of the index to tell you the real story.

### Positive Breadth
When most stocks on the NGX are advancing (going up), it means the rally has broad participation. This is typically a sign of a healthy, sustainable bull market. If 150 stocks go up and only 40 go down, the market is genuinely strong.

### Negative Breadth
When most stocks are declining while the index looks flat or only slightly down, it could mean trouble is brewing beneath the surface. A narrow market where only a few blue chips hold up the index while smaller stocks are falling is often an early warning sign.

## How Market Breadth is Measured

There are several ways to measure breadth:

### 1. Advancers vs. Decliners
The simplest method. Every trading day on the NGX, the exchange reports how many stocks gained (advancers), lost (decliners), and stayed unchanged. If advancers significantly outnumber decliners, breadth is positive.

### 2. Advance/Decline Ratio
This is the advancers divided by decliners. A ratio above 1.0 means more stocks went up than down.

### 3. Advance/Decline Line
A running total that adds advancers and subtracts decliners each day. If this line is trending upward, the broader market is healthy.

### 4. New Highs vs. New Lows
How many stocks are hitting 52-week highs versus 52-week lows. More new highs signal broad strength.

## Reading Breadth on the NGX

On a typical NGX trading day, you might see a report like:

- **Gainers:** 32 stocks
- **Losers:** 18 stocks
- **Unchanged:** 75 stocks

This shows moderately positive breadth — more stocks went up than down, though many did not move at all (which is common on the NGX, especially among smaller stocks).

The NGX daily market report, available on the NGX website and through broker platforms like **ARM Securities** and **Meristem**, includes this data.

## What This Means for You

Do not just look at the ASI number and assume the market is doing well or poorly. Check market breadth to see if the move is genuine and broad-based. A rising ASI with poor breadth is like a parade where only two people are marching — it might not last. Platforms like **Bamboo** and **Trove** show daily market summaries, and broker research from **Stanbic IBTC** and **Chapel Hill Denham** often includes breadth analysis.
