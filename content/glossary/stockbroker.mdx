Imagine you want to buy a plot of land in Lekki. You cannot just walk up to the land and claim it — you need an estate agent who knows the market, handles the paperwork, and makes sure the transaction is legitimate. A **stockbroker** is your estate agent for the stock market. They are the licensed professional or firm that buys and sells shares on your behalf on the Nigerian Exchange (NGX).

In Nigeria, you cannot trade directly on the NGX. Every transaction must go through a broker registered with the **Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)** and authorized as a dealing member of the exchange.

## What Does a Stockbroker Do?

Your stockbroker wears many hats:

- **Executes your trades** — when you say "buy 500 shares of Dangote Cement," your broker places that order on the NGX trading system
- **Opens your CSCS account** — your broker handles the paperwork to set up your share depository account and CHN number
- **Provides research and advice** — many brokers publish reports on companies, sectors, and market trends to help you make better decisions
- **Manages portfolios** — some brokers offer managed accounts where professionals invest on your behalf based on your goals

## Types of Stockbrokers in Nigeria

### Traditional Full-Service Brokers

These are established firms with physical offices, dedicated relationship managers, and deep research teams. They are ideal if you value personal attention and professional advice.

- **ARM Securities** — one of Nigeria's most respected investment firms with decades of experience
- **Meristem Stockbrokers** — known for award-winning research and strong client service
- **CardinalStone Securities** — popular with both retail and institutional investors
- **Stanbic IBTC Stockbrokers** — backed by the Standard Bank Group, one of Africa's largest financial institutions
- **Chapel Hill Denham** — strong across equities, fixed income, and wealth management
- **CSL Stockbrokers (FCMB)** — offers both online and traditional brokerage services

### Digital-First Brokers (Fintech Platforms)

These are mobile apps designed for a new generation of investors. They make stock buying as simple as ordering food on a delivery app.

- **Bamboo** — sleek app with access to both NGX and US stocks. Great for beginners. Low minimum investment
- **Trove** — strong focus on the Nigerian market with good educational content and research tools
- **Chaka** — SEC-licensed platform offering Nigerian and global stocks with a user-friendly interface

These platforms still use licensed brokerage infrastructure behind the scenes, so your investments are legitimate and regulated.

## Brokerage Fees — What You Will Pay

Nothing is free, including stock trading. Here is what typical fees look like on the NGX:

| Fee Type | Typical Rate | Who Charges It |
|---|---|---|
| Brokerage commission | 0.75% – 1.35% | Your broker |
| SEC fee | 0.3% | Securities and Exchange Commission |
| NGX fee | 0.3% | Nigerian Exchange |
| CSCS fee | 0.3% | Central Securities Clearing System |

So if you buy ₦100,000 worth of shares, total fees might be around ₦2,000 to ₦3,000. Digital apps like Bamboo and Trove often simplify this into a single transparent fee.

**Important:** Fees are charged on both buying and selling, so factor this into your investment calculations.

## How to Choose the Right Broker

Picking a broker is like choosing a bank — it depends on what matters most to you:

- **If you want simplicity and speed** — go with a digital app like Bamboo, Trove, or Chaka
- **If you want personal advice and research** — choose a full-service broker like Meristem, ARM Securities, or CardinalStone
- **If you are investing large sums** — a traditional broker with a dedicated relationship manager makes sense
- **If you want the lowest fees** — compare fee schedules across platforms; digital brokers tend to be cheaper

Always verify that your broker is **SEC-registered**. You can check the SEC Nigeria website for a list of licensed dealing members.

## What This Means for You

Your stockbroker is your most important partner in the stock market. For most beginners in 2026, starting with a digital app like Bamboo or Trove is the easiest path — you can be trading within a day. As your portfolio grows and your needs become more complex, you might add a traditional broker for advisory services. The key is to start with someone you trust and whose fees you understand.
