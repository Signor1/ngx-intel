Imagine you and four friends buy a popcorn machine together. At the end of the month, the machine has earned ₦50,000 in profit. You all agree to share ₦30,000 among yourselves and keep ₦20,000 to buy more ingredients. Your share of that ₦30,000 is your **dividend** — a reward for owning part of the business.

A **dividend** is a portion of a company's profit that is paid out to shareholders. When a company listed on the Nigerian Exchange (NGX) makes money and decides to share some of it, every person who owns shares gets a piece based on how many shares they hold.

## How Dividends Work on the NGX — A Real Story

Let us say Amina, a teacher in Abuja, bought 2,000 shares of **GTCO** at ₦28 per share in January 2025, spending ₦56,000. Later that year, GTCO's board announced a final dividend of ₦3.00 per share. Without selling a single share, Amina received ₦6,000 deposited straight into the bank account linked to her CSCS account. That is money earned just for holding the stock — like rent from a property, but from a company.

## Who Pays Dividends on the NGX?

Not every company pays dividends. Companies that consistently share profits with shareholders are often called **dividend aristocrats**. On the NGX, some of the most reliable dividend payers include:

- **Zenith Bank** — has paid dividends every year for over a decade
- **GTCO (Guaranty Trust Holding Company)** — known for generous payouts
- **Stanbic IBTC** — a consistent performer
- **MTN Nigeria** — joined the dividend club after its listing
- **Dangote Cement** — Africa's largest cement producer shares profits regularly
- **Seplat Energy** — pays dividends in both Naira and US Dollars

Growth-stage companies, on the other hand, may choose to reinvest all profits back into the business instead of paying dividends. This is not necessarily bad — it means they believe reinvesting will create more value in the long run.

## How You Receive Your Dividend

Once a dividend is declared and you own shares before the **ex-dividend date**, the money is paid directly into the bank account linked to your **CSCS account**. The process is automatic. If you use platforms like **Bamboo**, **Trove**, or **Chaka**, dividends are credited to your wallet or linked account.

Dividends in Nigeria are subject to a **10% withholding tax**, which is deducted at source. So if your dividend is ₦10,000, you will receive ₦9,000 after tax.

## The Two Ways to Make Money from Stocks

Dividends are one of two main ways to earn from the stock market:

1. **Dividends** — regular cash payments while you hold the stock (like collecting rent)
2. **Capital gains** — selling your shares at a higher price than you bought them (like selling a house for more than you paid)

Smart investors on the NGX often combine both strategies, picking stocks that pay solid dividends *and* have room to grow in price.

## What This Means for You

If you are a beginner looking for a steady way to earn from the stock market without constantly checking prices, dividend-paying stocks are worth exploring. Even a small portfolio of ₦100,000 spread across three or four reliable dividend payers on the NGX can start generating passive income while you learn the ropes. Over time, reinvesting those dividends to buy more shares creates a powerful snowball effect.
