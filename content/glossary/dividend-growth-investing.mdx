Imagine you plant a mango tree in your compound. The first year, it produces 10 mangoes. The second year, 25. By the fifth year, it gives you 200 mangoes every season. You did not do anything extra — the tree just grew, and its output kept increasing. **Dividend growth investing** is like planting financial mango trees — buying shares in companies that increase their dividends year after year, so your income keeps growing without you investing additional money.

**Dividend growth investing** is a long-term strategy focused on buying shares of companies that not only pay dividends but consistently **increase** those dividends over time. The goal is not necessarily the highest yield today, but the fastest growing yield over years and decades.

## Why Growth Matters More Than Current Yield

Here is the key insight that separates beginners from experienced dividend investors:

**Company A:** Pays a 10% dividend yield but has not increased its dividend in 5 years
**Company B:** Pays a 6% dividend yield but has increased its dividend by 15% every year for the past 5 years

Which is better? Let us run the numbers on a ₦100,000 investment:

| Year | Company A Income | Company B Income |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | ₦10,000 | ₦6,000 |
| Year 3 | ₦10,000 | ₦7,935 |
| Year 5 | ₦10,000 | ₦10,456 |
| Year 7 | ₦10,000 | ₦13,810 |
| Year 10 | ₦10,000 | ₦21,304 |

By year 5, Company B catches up. By year 10, it is paying you more than double what Company A pays. And the gap keeps widening. This is the power of dividend growth.

## How to Build a Dividend Growth Portfolio on the NGX

### Step 1: Find Companies That Grow Dividends
Look at the dividend history over at least 5 years. Is the dividend per share increasing most years? You can find this data in annual reports or through research from **Meristem**, **ARM Securities**, or **CardinalStone**.

### Step 2: Check the Fundamentals
A sustainable dividend grower needs:
- **Growing earnings (EPS)** — dividends come from profits, so profits must grow
- **Moderate payout ratio** (30-60%) — the company needs room to keep increasing dividends
- **Strong ROE** (above 15%) — shows efficient use of capital
- **Manageable debt** — heavy debt limits a company's ability to raise dividends

### Step 3: Buy and Hold Patiently
Dividend growth investing is not a get-rich-quick scheme. It is a get-rich-slowly-but-surely strategy. Buy quality companies, reinvest your dividends to buy more shares, and let compounding work its magic over 10, 15, or 20 years.

### Step 4: Reinvest Dividends
This is where the strategy becomes truly powerful. When you receive ₦10,000 in dividends, use it to buy more shares. Those extra shares will earn their own dividends, which buy even more shares. Over many years, this snowball effect can turn a modest initial investment into a significant income stream.

## The Yield-on-Cost Magic

**Yield-on-cost** is your current dividend income divided by your original purchase price. It reveals the true power of dividend growth investing.

Example: You bought GTCO at ₦15 per share ten years ago. Today the company pays ₦3.00 per share in dividends. Your yield-on-cost is (3.00 / 15) x 100 = **20%**. You are earning 20% per year on your original investment — every year — just from dividends. Plus, the share price has likely appreciated significantly too.
