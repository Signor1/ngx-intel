Picture Balogun Market on a busy Saturday versus a quiet Tuesday morning. On Saturday, thousands of people are buying and selling — the market is buzzing with activity. On Tuesday, only a handful of traders are around. **Trading volume** is how we measure this level of activity on the stock market.

**Volume** refers to the total number of shares of a stock that are bought and sold during a specific period, usually a single trading day. If 3 million shares of **MTN Nigeria** are traded on Monday, the daily volume is 3 million. Each transaction has a buyer and a seller, so volume counts the total shares that changed hands.

## Why Volume Matters

Volume tells you important things about a stock:

### 1. Liquidity
High volume means a stock is **liquid** — there are plenty of buyers and sellers, so you can get in and out easily. On the NGX, blue chip stocks like **Dangote Cement**, **GTCO**, and **Zenith Bank** consistently have the highest volume. You can buy or sell thousands of shares without significantly affecting the price.

Low volume stocks are the opposite. If a stock only trades 5,000 shares per day and you want to sell 20,000, you might struggle to find enough buyers, or you might have to accept a lower price.

### 2. Confirmation of Price Moves
When a stock price goes up on **high volume**, it suggests the move is strong and supported by many investors. Think of it like a crowd: if a large crowd is moving in one direction, the momentum is real. If only a few people are moving, the movement might not last.

Conversely, if a stock drops on low volume, the decline might not be serious — there just were not many sellers, and a few trades pushed the price down.

### 3. Interest and Sentiment
A sudden spike in volume often means something important is happening — an earnings announcement, a dividend declaration, a news event, or a major investor making a move. When volume in a normally quiet stock suddenly jumps from 50,000 to 2 million shares in a day, something is going on.

| MTN Nigeria | 1 – 5 million shares |

| Smaller companies | Under 100,000 shares |

These numbers fluctuate, but they give you a sense of scale. The entire NGX might see 200 million to 500 million shares traded on an active day.

## How to Use Volume as a Beginner

Here are two simple rules:

1. **Prefer stocks with decent volume** — if you are just starting out, stick with stocks that trade at least a few hundred thousand shares daily. This ensures you can buy and sell when you want to.

2. **Watch for volume spikes** — if a stock you own suddenly has 10 times its normal volume, check the news. It could be an opportunity or a warning.

## What This Means for You

You can check daily trading volumes on platforms like **Bamboo**, **Trove**, **Chaka**, or the NGX website. As a beginner, volume helps you avoid getting stuck in a stock nobody wants to buy. Stick with actively traded stocks and use volume as a companion to price when making investment decisions.
