Imagine your favourite restaurant runs a promo: "Buy 10 meals, get 1 free." You did not pay extra — the restaurant simply rewarded your loyalty with a bonus. **Bonus shares** work exactly the same way on the stock market. A company gives you extra shares for free, based on how many you already own.

**Bonus shares** (also called a bonus issue or scrip issue) are additional shares a company gives to existing shareholders at no cost. Instead of paying a cash dividend, the company rewards you with more shares. It is the company's way of saying "thank you for being a shareholder" using shares instead of Naira.

## How Bonus Shares Work

The bonus is expressed as a ratio. Common examples:

- **1 for 5** — you get 1 new share for every 5 you already own
- **1 for 10** — you get 1 new share for every 10 you own
- **1 for 2** — you get 1 new share for every 2 you own (a very generous bonus)

### A Nigerian Example

Let us say you own 5,000 shares of **Zenith Bank** at ₦35 per share. Your holdings are worth ₦175,000. Zenith Bank declares a **1-for-5 bonus issue**. You automatically receive 1,000 additional shares (5,000 / 5 = 1,000), bringing your total to 6,000 shares — without spending a single Naira.

But here is the important part: the share price typically adjusts downward to reflect the bonus. If the stock was ₦35 before the bonus, it might drop to about ₦29.17 (₦175,000 / 6,000 shares). The total value of your holdings stays roughly the same immediately after the bonus. You have more slices, but each slice is proportionally smaller.

## Why Do Companies Issue Bonus Shares?

Companies issue bonus shares for several reasons:

- **Reward shareholders without paying cash** — the company keeps its cash for business operations while still giving investors something
- **Make shares more affordable** — when a stock price gets very high, a bonus issue brings the price down, making it easier for small investors to buy in
- **Signal confidence** — a bonus issue tells the market that the company is confident about its future earnings
- **Increase liquidity** — more shares in circulation means more trading activity

## Famous Bonus Issues on the NGX

Several NGX companies have a history of generous bonus issues:

- **Zenith Bank** regularly combines cash dividends with bonus share issues
- **Nestlé Nigeria** has issued bonuses that rewarded patient investors handsomely

## Are Bonus Shares Actually "Free Money"?

Not exactly. Right after a bonus issue, the total value of your investment does not change — you just own more shares at a lower price. However, over time, if the company continues to grow and the share price recovers to its pre-bonus level, your bonus shares become genuine gains. Think of it as planting extra seeds that will bear fruit in the future.
