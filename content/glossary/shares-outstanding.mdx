Imagine you bake a large cake and cut it into 100 slices. Those 100 slices are the total pieces of your cake that exist. In the stock market, **shares outstanding** is the total number of slices (shares) a company has been cut into — including those held by the founders, big investors, and everyday people like you.

**Shares outstanding** refers to the total number of a company's shares that are currently held by all shareholders. This includes shares held by company insiders (founders, executives), institutional investors (pension funds, banks), and retail investors (individual people).

## Why Does This Number Matter?

Shares outstanding is the foundation for several important calculations:

### 1. Market Capitalisation
**Market cap = Share price x Shares outstanding**

If **MTN Nigeria** has 20.4 billion shares outstanding and the stock price is ₦260, then MTN's market capitalisation is ₦260 x 20.4 billion = approximately ₦5.3 trillion. This tells you the total value the market places on the entire company.

### 2. Earnings Per Share (EPS)
**EPS = Net profit / Shares outstanding**

If a company makes ₦100 billion in profit and has 10 billion shares outstanding, the EPS is ₦10. This tells you how much profit each share earned.

### 3. Dividends Per Share
If a company decides to distribute ₦50 billion in dividends and has 10 billion shares outstanding, each share gets ₦5.

## Can the Number Change?

Yes. Shares outstanding is not fixed forever. The number can increase or decrease:

- **It increases** when a company issues new shares through a **rights issue**, **bonus shares**, or an **IPO**
- **It decreases** when a company buys back its own shares (called a **share buyback**)

For example, when **Access Bank** conducted a rights issue, it created new shares and sold them to existing shareholders, increasing the total shares outstanding.

## What This Means for You

When you see a stock with a very low price, do not assume it is cheap — check the shares outstanding. A stock trading at ₦2 with 50 billion shares outstanding represents a ₦100 billion company, which is not small at all. Always look at the full picture by combining share price with shares outstanding to understand a company's true size. You can find this information on platforms like **Bamboo**, **Trove**, or the NGX website.
