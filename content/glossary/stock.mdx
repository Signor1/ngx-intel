A **stock** (also called a **share**) represents a unit of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock of a company listed on the Nigerian Exchange (NGX), you become a part-owner of that company. The more shares you own, the larger your ownership stake.

## How It Works

Companies sell shares to raise money for growth, expansion, or operations. In Nigeria, major companies like **Dangote Cement**, **MTN Nigeria**, **GTCO**, and **BUA Cement** have millions of shares available for the public to buy and sell on the NGX.

For example, if a company has 1,000,000 total shares and you own 10,000 of them, you own 1% of that company. As a shareholder, you have certain rights, including the right to vote at Annual General Meetings (AGMs) and the right to receive dividends when they are declared.

## Types of Shares on the NGX

The most common type of share traded on the NGX is the **ordinary share** (also called common stock). Ordinary shareholders can vote on company matters and receive dividends. Some companies also issue **preference shares**, which give holders priority when dividends are paid but usually do not carry voting rights.

## How You Make Money from Stocks

There are two main ways to earn from stocks:

1. **Capital gains** - buying a share at a lower price and selling it at a higher price. If you buy MTN Nigeria at ₦180 and sell at ₦220, your capital gain is ₦40 per share.
2. **Dividends** - receiving a share of the company's profits paid out to shareholders.

## Key Takeaway

Owning a stock means owning a piece of a real business. The Nigerian stock market gives everyday Nigerians the opportunity to invest in some of the biggest companies in Africa.
