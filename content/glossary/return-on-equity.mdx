Imagine two friends each start a business. Amara invested ₦5 million and made ₦1 million profit. Bola invested ₦20 million and also made ₦1 million profit. Who is doing a better job with their money? Amara — she turned every ₦5 into ₦1 of profit, while Bola needed ₦20 to do the same. **Return on Equity (ROE)** measures exactly this: how efficiently a company uses its shareholders' money to generate profits.

**Return on Equity (ROE)** is a profitability ratio that measures how much net income a company generates for every Naira of shareholders' equity. It tells you how good a company is at turning the money invested by shareholders into profit.

**Formula:** ROE = (Net Income / Shareholders' Equity) x 100

## Calculating ROE — An NGX Example

### GTCO
- Net income: ₦200 billion
- Shareholders' equity: ₦900 billion
- **ROE: (200 / 900) x 100 = 22.2%**

This means for every ₦100 of shareholders' money, GTCO generates ₦22.20 in profit. That is strong performance.

### A Weaker Performer
- Net income: ₦15 billion
- Shareholders' equity: ₦300 billion
- **ROE: (15 / 300) x 100 = 5.0%**

Only ₦5 of profit for every ₦100 of equity. The company is not using shareholders' money very effectively.

## What Good ROE Looks Like on the NGX

| ROE Range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Below 5% | Poor — the company is struggling to generate adequate returns |
| 5% – 10% | Below average — may be underperforming peers |
| 10% – 15% | Average — acceptable for many industries |
| 15% – 25% | Good to excellent — well-managed, profitable company |
| Above 25% | Outstanding — but check if high debt is inflating the number |

Nigerian banking stocks tend to have some of the highest ROEs on the NGX. Top-tier banks like **GTCO**, **Zenith Bank**, and **Stanbic IBTC** frequently report ROEs above 20%. Consumer goods companies like **Nestlé Nigeria** also show strong ROEs due to their powerful brands and pricing power.

## Why ROE is So Useful

### 1. Comparing Companies Within a Sector
ROE is most powerful when you compare companies in the same industry. If you are choosing between two Nigerian banks, comparing their ROEs tells you which management team is more efficient at generating profits from the capital available.

### 2. Identifying Quality Stocks
Companies with consistently high ROE (above 15% for several years) are typically well-managed businesses with competitive advantages. These are the kinds of stocks that tend to reward long-term investors.

### 3. Spotting Red Flags
A suddenly falling ROE might signal trouble — declining profits, asset write-downs, or operational problems. Conversely, a rising ROE suggests the company is getting more efficient.

## The Debt Warning

There is one important caveat: a company can artificially inflate its ROE by taking on a lot of debt. More debt means less equity on the balance sheet, which makes the ROE formula produce a higher number. Always check the **debt-to-equity ratio** alongside ROE. A company with an ROE of 30% but a dangerously high debt load is riskier than one with an ROE of 18% and minimal debt.

## What This Means for You

ROE is one of the first numbers experienced investors check when evaluating a stock. Make it a habit to compare the ROE of any NGX company you are considering buying with its sector peers. Look for companies that consistently deliver ROE above 15%. You can find ROE data in annual reports, on the NGX website, and through research from **Meristem**, **ARM Securities**, **CardinalStone**, and **Chapel Hill Denham**.
