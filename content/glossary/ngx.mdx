The **NGX (Nigerian Exchange Limited)** is Nigeria's principal securities exchange where stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments are bought and sold. It is the marketplace where investors trade shares of publicly listed Nigerian companies. The NGX is headquartered in Lagos and is one of the largest stock exchanges in Africa.

## A Brief History

The exchange was originally founded in 1960 as the **Lagos Stock Exchange** and was later renamed the **Nigerian Stock Exchange (NSE)**. In 2021, it underwent a restructuring and became **Nigerian Exchange Limited (NGX)**, operating as a subsidiary of the **Nigerian Exchange Group (NGX Group)**.

## What Is Listed on the NGX?

The NGX hosts over 150 listed companies across multiple sectors including banking, oil and gas, consumer goods, industrial goods, and telecommunications. Some of the most actively traded companies include **Dangote Cement**, **MTN Nigeria**, **Zenith Bank**, **GTCO**, **BUA Cement**, and **Airtel Africa**.

Beyond equities (stocks), the NGX also facilitates trading in **bonds** (including Federal Government bonds), **exchange-traded funds (ETFs)**, and **memorandum listings**.

## How Trading Works

The NGX operates a fully electronic trading platform. Trading takes place on business days from **10:00 AM to 2:30 PM** (West African Time). All trades are settled through the **Central Securities Clearing System (CSCS)**, which acts as the central depository for all securities traded on the exchange.

## Why the NGX Matters

The NGX plays a critical role in Nigeria's economy. It allows companies to raise capital for expansion and gives ordinary Nigerians the opportunity to build wealth by investing in the country's leading businesses. The exchange is regulated by the **Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) Nigeria**.
