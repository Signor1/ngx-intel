Think about the price of tomatoes in Nigeria throughout the year. During harvest season, a basket might cost ₦3,000 (the yearly low). During scarcity, that same basket might shoot up to ₦15,000 (the yearly high). The **52-week high and low** of a stock works the same way — it tracks the highest and lowest prices a stock has reached over the past 52 weeks (one year).

The **52-week high** is the highest price a stock has traded at during the past year. The **52-week low** is the lowest price it has traded at in that same period. Together, they create a price range that shows you how much the stock has moved.

## Why 52-Week Highs and Lows Matter

### 1. Context for the Current Price
If **GTCO** is trading at ₦42 and its 52-week range is ₦28 to ₦48, you can see that the stock is closer to its yearly high. It has already moved up significantly. If it is at ₦30, it is closer to the low — possibly an opportunity if the fundamentals are still strong.

### 2. Identifying Momentum
Stocks hitting **new 52-week highs** often have strong momentum. When **Dangote Cement** or **MTN Nigeria** breaks through to a new high, it signals that investor confidence is strong and demand is outweighing supply.

Conversely, stocks at **52-week lows** might be going through tough times — but they could also represent bargains if the problems are temporary.

### 3. Psychological Barriers
The 52-week high often acts as a psychological **resistance level** — many investors hesitate to buy a stock at its highest price in a year. The 52-week low acts as a **support level** — investors see it as a floor and step in to buy, believing it cannot go much lower.

## How to Use 52-Week Data on the NGX

### Strategy 1: Buying Near the 52-Week Low
If a fundamentally strong company is trading near its 52-week low due to temporary market conditions (not because the company itself is in trouble), it could be a buying opportunity. For example, if **Zenith Bank** drops near its yearly low during a broad market sell-off, but its earnings and dividends remain strong, value investors might see this as a discount.

### Strategy 2: Watching 52-Week High Breakouts
When a stock breaks above its 52-week high on strong volume, it can signal the beginning of a new upward trend. Momentum investors watch for these breakouts as entry signals.

### Strategy 3: Measuring Volatility
A stock with a 52-week range of ₦25 to ₦30 (₦5 range) is much less volatile than one with a range of ₦100 to ₦300 (₦200 range). The wider the range, the more the stock has swung — which means both more risk and more opportunity.

## Where to Find 52-Week Data

- **NGX website** — shows 52-week highs and lows for all listed stocks
- **Bamboo, Trove, and Chaka** — display price charts where you can visually see the range
- **Broker research reports** from **Meristem**, **Stanbic IBTC**, or **ARM Securities** include this data
- **Financial news sites** covering the NGX

## What This Means for You

The 52-week high/low is a quick way to put any stock's current price in perspective. Before buying a stock, check where it sits within its yearly range. If you are buying near the high, make sure the fundamentals justify the premium. If you are buying near the low, make sure the company is not in genuine trouble. It is a simple but powerful tool in your investing toolkit.
