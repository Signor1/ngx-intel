The **All-Share Index (ASI)** is the main benchmark index of the Nigerian Exchange (NGX). It tracks the general performance of all listed companies on the exchange and serves as the primary indicator of how the Nigerian stock market is doing overall.

## How the ASI Works

The ASI is calculated using the **market capitalisation** of every listed company on the NGX. When the share prices of listed companies rise on average, the ASI goes up. When they fall, the ASI goes down. Think of it as a single number that summarises the entire market's movement for the day.

The index started with a base value of **100 points** on January 3, 1984. Over the decades, it has grown significantly. For context, the ASI has traded well above **50,000 points** in recent years, reflecting the growth of Nigerian listed companies.

## Why the ASI Matters

The ASI is the first thing most Nigerian investors and analysts look at each trading day. It answers a simple question: **"Did the market go up or down today?"**

If you hear someone say "the market gained 1.5% today," they are referring to the ASI. Financial news outlets like **NGXIntel**, Bloomberg, and Nairametrics report the daily ASI movement to keep investors informed.

## ASI vs Sector Indices

While the ASI covers the entire market, the NGX also publishes **sector indices** for banking, consumer goods, oil and gas, industrial goods, and insurance. These help investors track performance within specific sectors. For instance, if bank stocks are rallying but oil stocks are falling, the ASI might remain flat even though individual sectors are moving.

## Key Takeaway

The ASI is the heartbeat of the Nigerian stock market. Watching its daily movement gives you a quick snapshot of investor sentiment and the overall direction of the market.
