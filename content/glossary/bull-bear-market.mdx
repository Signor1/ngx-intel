The terms **bull market** and **bear market** describe the general direction of the stock market over a period of time. They are among the most commonly used phrases in investing and you will hear them frequently in discussions about the Nigerian Exchange (NGX).

## What Is a Bull Market?

A **bull market** is a period when stock prices are rising or are expected to rise. During a bull market, investor confidence is high, the economy is generally strong, and more people are buying stocks. The NGX has experienced several bull runs, notably between 2020 and 2024, when the All-Share Index (ASI) climbed significantly, driven by strong performances from companies like **MTN Nigeria**, **Dangote Cement**, and **GTCO**.

In a bull market, the prevailing sentiment is optimism. Investors are eager to buy, and even first-time investors are drawn to the market by rising prices and positive news.

## What Is a Bear Market?

A **bear market** is the opposite. It occurs when stock prices are falling or expected to fall, typically by 20% or more from recent highs. During a bear market, investor confidence drops, selling pressure increases, and fear dominates. Nigeria experienced a notable bear market in 2008-2009 following the global financial crisis, when the ASI lost over 60% of its value.

Bear markets can be triggered by economic downturns, rising inflation, currency devaluation, or political instability.

## How to Navigate Both Markets

- **In a bull market**, focus on quality stocks with strong fundamentals. Avoid chasing stocks that have already risen too far too fast.
- **In a bear market**, look for opportunities to buy solid companies at discounted prices. Historically, bear markets have been some of the best times to invest for long-term gains.

## Key Takeaway

Markets naturally cycle between bull and bear phases. Understanding which phase the NGX is in helps you make more informed investment decisions, whether that means buying aggressively or exercising patience.
