Imagine you invested ₦200,000 in your friend's pepper soup joint. At the end of the year, you would want to know: How much money did we make? How much did we spend? Are we growing or struggling? What are the plans for next year? An **annual report** is exactly that — a detailed yearly account of how a company performed.

An **annual report** is a comprehensive document published by a company every year that covers its financial performance, operations, strategy, and outlook. It is the most important document an investor can read to understand whether a company is healthy, growing, and worth investing in.

## What is Inside an Annual Report?

Annual reports on the NGX typically contain these key sections:

### 1. Chairman's Statement
A letter from the chairman summarising the year — major achievements, challenges, and vision for the future. It gives you a sense of leadership and direction.

### 2. Financial Statements
This is the heart of the report. It includes:
- **Income Statement (Profit & Loss)** — how much money the company made and spent. Revenue, costs, and net profit
- **Balance Sheet** — what the company owns (assets) versus what it owes (liabilities)
- **Cash Flow Statement** — how cash moved in and out of the business

### 3. Directors' Report
Details about board decisions, governance, and management team changes.

### 4. Auditor's Report
An independent auditor (like PwC, KPMG, Deloitte, or Ernst & Young) reviews the accounts and confirms whether they are accurate and fair. This is your assurance that the numbers are trustworthy.

### 5. Notes to the Financial Statements
Detailed explanations behind the numbers. This is where you find the real story — accounting policies, breakdowns of revenue by segment, details on loans, and more.

## How to Read an Annual Report (The Beginner's Shortcut)

You do not need to read all 200 pages. Here is what to focus on first:

1. **Revenue** — is it growing year over year? A company that makes more money each year is heading in the right direction
2. **Net profit** — after all expenses, how much profit is left? Consistent profits are a good sign
3. **Earnings per share (EPS)** — how much profit each share earned. Compare it to previous years
4. **Dividend declared** — how much is the company returning to shareholders?
5. **Debt levels** — is the company borrowing heavily? Compare total debt to total equity

### A Quick Example: GTCO's Annual Report

If GTCO reports revenue of ₦600 billion, net profit of ₦200 billion, and EPS of ₦6.80 — and all three numbers are higher than last year — that tells you the company is growing. If they also declared a ₦3.00 dividend, you know shareholders are being rewarded.

## Where to Find Annual Reports

Annual reports for NGX-listed companies are available for free:

- **Company websites** — most listed companies have an "Investor Relations" section
- **NGX website** (ngxgroup.com) — filings and disclosures from all listed companies
- **SEC Nigeria website** — regulatory filings
- **Your broker's research section** — platforms like **ARM Securities**, **Meristem**, and **CardinalStone** often provide summaries and analysis

Some digital platforms like **Bamboo** and **Trove** also provide simplified financial data from annual reports within their apps.

## What This Means for You

Before investing in any company on the NGX, check its latest annual report. You do not need to be an accountant — just look at the trends. Is revenue growing? Is profit increasing? Is debt manageable? Are dividends being paid? Spending 30 minutes reading the highlights of an annual report could save you from investing in a struggling company or confirm that you have found a winner.
