**Dividend yield** is a financial ratio that tells you how much a company pays in dividends relative to its share price. It is expressed as a percentage and helps investors compare the income they can earn from different stocks.

## The Formula

> **Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividend Per Share / Current Share Price) x 100**

## A Nigerian Example

Let us say **Zenith Bank** pays an annual dividend of ₦3.50 per share and its current share price on the NGX is ₦35.00. The dividend yield would be:

**Dividend Yield = (₦3.50 / ₦35.00) x 100 = 10%**

This means that for every Naira you invest in Zenith Bank shares, you earn 10 kobo in dividends per year. Compare this to another stock that might yield only 3% and you can quickly see which one offers more income.

## Why Dividend Yield Matters

Dividend yield is one of the most popular metrics used by Nigerian investors to evaluate stocks. A high dividend yield can be attractive, but it is important to look deeper. Sometimes a high yield occurs because the share price has dropped significantly, which could signal trouble with the company.

On the NGX, banks and consumer goods companies like **GTCO**, **Stanbic IBTC**, and **Nestle Nigeria** tend to have notable dividend yields. Comparing dividend yields helps you decide where to allocate your capital if earning regular income is your goal.

## Key Takeaway

Dividend yield is a quick way to assess how much return you get from dividends alone. Always compare it alongside other metrics like the P/E ratio and earnings per share for a fuller picture.
