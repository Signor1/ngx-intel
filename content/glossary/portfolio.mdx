A **portfolio** is the collection of all the investments you own. If you hold shares in multiple companies on the Nigerian Exchange (NGX), those shares together make up your stock portfolio. A portfolio can also include other asset classes like bonds, mutual funds, real estate, and savings, but in the context of the NGX, it primarily refers to your stock holdings.

## Building a Portfolio on the NGX

Suppose you invest in the following stocks:

- 500 shares of **Zenith Bank** at ₦35 each
- 200 shares of **MTN Nigeria** at ₦200 each
- 100 shares of **Dangote Cement** at ₦290 each

Your portfolio would be worth: (500 x ₦35) + (200 x ₦200) + (100 x ₦290) = ₦17,500 + ₦40,000 + ₦29,000 = **₦86,500**

This collection of three stocks across banking, telecoms, and industrial goods sectors is your portfolio.

## Diversification

One of the most important principles in portfolio management is **diversification**, which means spreading your investments across different companies and sectors. If all your money is in one stock and that company performs poorly, your entire investment suffers. By owning stocks in banking (Zenith Bank), telecoms (MTN), and cement (Dangote), you reduce your overall risk.

A well-diversified Nigerian portfolio might include stocks from sectors like **banking**, **consumer goods**, **oil and gas**, **telecoms**, and **industrial goods**.

## Monitoring Your Portfolio

Most Nigerian stockbrokers and investment apps provide portfolio tracking tools that show you your holdings, their current market value, and your profit or loss. Regularly reviewing your portfolio helps you make informed decisions about when to buy more, sell, or hold.

## Key Takeaway

Your portfolio is your personal collection of investments. Building a well-diversified portfolio across different sectors on the NGX is one of the best strategies for managing risk and growing your wealth over time.
