Imagine you earn ₦500,000 per month. You decide to give ₦150,000 to your family and keep ₦350,000 for savings, bills, and personal expenses. The percentage you give away — 30% — is your "payout ratio." Companies do the same thing with their profits: some goes to shareholders as dividends, and the rest is kept for business growth.

The **payout ratio** tells you what percentage of a company's earnings is being paid out to shareholders as dividends. It is one of the most important numbers for dividend investors because it reveals whether a company's dividend is sustainable or if the company is stretching itself too thin.

**Formula:** Payout Ratio = (Total Dividends Paid / Net Income) x 100

## How to Calculate the Payout Ratio

### Example 1: Zenith Bank
- Net income: ₦280 billion
- Total dividends paid: ₦110 billion
- Payout ratio: (110 / 280) x 100 = **39.3%**

This means Zenith Bank paid out about 39% of its profits and retained 61% for growth and reserves. That is a healthy, sustainable ratio.

### Example 2: A Hypothetical Company
- Net income: ₦50 billion
- Total dividends paid: ₦48 billion
- Payout ratio: (48 / 50) x 100 = **96%**

This company is paying out almost everything it earns. There is very little left for reinvestment, emergencies, or growth. If profits dip even slightly, the dividend might be cut.

## What the Numbers Tell You

| Payout Ratio | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 0% – 30% | Conservative — company is reinvesting heavily in growth |
| 30% – 60% | Balanced — healthy mix of dividends and reinvestment |
| 60% – 80% | Generous — mature company prioritising shareholder returns |
| 80% – 100% | Aggressive — very little retained for growth, dividend could be at risk |
| Over 100% | Danger zone — paying more in dividends than it earns |

A payout ratio **over 100%** means the company is dipping into reserves or borrowing to pay dividends. This is not sustainable long-term. If you see this on an NGX stock, be cautious.

## Payout Ratios of Nigerian Companies

Nigerian banks tend to have moderate payout ratios, typically in the 30-50% range. This is because the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) requires banks to maintain certain capital reserves.

Consumer goods companies like **Nestlé Nigeria** and **Unilever Nigeria** often have higher payout ratios (60-80%) because they are mature businesses with stable cash flows.

Oil and gas companies like **Seplat Energy** balance dividends with the need to invest in exploration and production, often landing in the 30-50% range.

## Why the Payout Ratio Matters More Than Dividend Yield

Many beginners chase high dividend yields without checking the payout ratio. A stock might have an attractive 15% yield, but if the payout ratio is 120%, that dividend is likely to be cut — and when dividends are cut, the share price usually drops too.

Think of it this way: dividend yield tells you how much you **are** getting paid. Payout ratio tells you how long the company **can keep** paying you.

## What This Means for You

Before buying any NGX stock for its dividend, check the payout ratio. A ratio between 30% and 60% is the sweet spot for most investors — it means the company is sharing profits generously while keeping enough to grow and handle tough times. You can calculate this using data from annual reports, the NGX website, or research reports from brokers like **ARM Securities**, **Meristem**, or **CardinalStone**.
