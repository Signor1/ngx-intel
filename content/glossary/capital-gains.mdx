You buy a piece of land in Epe for ₦2 million. Three years later, a new road is built nearby and the land is now worth ₦5 million. If you sell it, the ₦3 million profit is your **capital gain** — the money you made because the value of your asset went up. Stocks work exactly the same way.

A **capital gain** is the profit you make when you sell an investment for more than you paid for it. If you bought shares at one price and sold them at a higher price, the difference is your capital gain.

**Formula:** Capital Gain = Selling Price - Purchase Price

## A Nigerian Stock Market Example

Let us follow Tunde, an investor in Abuja:

- In January 2024, Tunde bought 2,000 shares of **GTCO** at ₦28 per share, spending ₦56,000
- By December 2025, GTCO's share price had risen to ₦45 per share
- Tunde sells all 2,000 shares for ₦90,000
- **Capital gain: ₦90,000 - ₦56,000 = ₦34,000**

That is a 60.7% return on his investment — just from the share price going up. On top of that, Tunde collected dividends along the way, making his total return even higher.

## Capital Gains vs. Dividends

Stocks give you two ways to make money:

| | Capital Gains | Dividends |
|---|---|---|
| How you earn | Selling shares at a higher price | Company pays you a share of profits |
| When you get paid | Only when you sell | Usually once or twice a year |
| Requires selling? | Yes | No |
| Analogy | Selling a house for a profit | Collecting rent on a house |

Smart investors on the NGX often benefit from both. A stock like **MTN Nigeria** might pay dividends while its share price also appreciates over time.

## Capital Losses — The Other Side

It would be dishonest not to mention the flip side. If you sell a stock for **less** than you paid, the difference is a **capital loss**. If Tunde had bought GTCO at ₦45 and the price fell to ₦30, selling would result in a ₦15 per share loss.

This is why investing in quality companies and holding for the long term is so important — it reduces the chances of locking in losses.

## Tax on Capital Gains in Nigeria

One of the most attractive things about investing on the NGX: as of 2026, **capital gains from the disposal of shares on the NGX are exempt from capital gains tax** for most retail investors. This is a significant incentive compared to other investments. However, tax laws can change, so always check the latest rules or consult a tax professional.

Dividends, on the other hand, are subject to a 10% withholding tax.

## Unrealised vs. Realised Gains

- **Unrealised gain** — your stock has gone up, but you have not sold yet. It is a "paper profit." If you bought Dangote Cement at ₦250 and it is now at ₦350, you have an unrealised gain of ₦100 per share.
- **Realised gain** — you have sold the stock and the profit is in your account. It is real money.

You only truly make or lose money when you sell. Until then, gains and losses exist only on paper.

## What This Means for You

Capital gains are the exciting part of stock investing — watching a stock you researched and bought grow in value over time. The best approach for beginners is to buy quality NGX companies at reasonable prices and hold them patiently. Time in the market beats timing the market. Track your gains and losses on apps like **Bamboo**, **Trove**, or **Chaka**, which show your portfolio performance in real time.
