**Market capitalisation** (or market cap) is the total value of a company's outstanding shares. It tells you how much the market thinks an entire company is worth at any given time. Market cap is one of the simplest and most widely used measures of a company's size.

## The Formula

> **Market Capitalisation = Current Share Price x Total Number of Shares Outstanding**

## A Nigerian Example

Let us say **Dangote Cement** has approximately 17 billion shares outstanding and the current share price is ₦290. The market capitalisation would be:

**₦290 x 17,000,000,000 = ₦4.93 trillion**

This makes Dangote Cement one of the largest companies by market cap on the NGX. Other large-cap companies include **MTN Nigeria**, **Airtel Africa**, **BUA Cement**, and **Zenith Bank**.

## Why Market Cap Matters

Market capitalisation helps investors classify companies into categories:

- **Large-cap**: Companies worth over ₦500 billion. These are typically well-established businesses like Dangote Cement and MTN Nigeria. They tend to be more stable but may grow more slowly.
- **Mid-cap**: Companies worth between ₦50 billion and ₦500 billion. These offer a balance between stability and growth potential.
- **Small-cap**: Companies worth below ₦50 billion. These carry more risk but can offer higher returns if the company grows.

The total market capitalisation of all companies on the NGX is often reported in the news alongside the ASI. It gives a sense of the overall size and health of the Nigerian equity market.

## Key Takeaway

Market cap tells you the size of a company in monetary terms. It is essential for comparing companies and understanding where they fit in the broader market.
